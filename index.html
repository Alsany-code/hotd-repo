Origin

House of the Dragon is a fantasy television series created by George R. R. Martin and Ryan Condal, and it premiered on August 21, 2022, on HBO. It is a prequel to the popular series Game of Thrones and is based on George R. R. Martin's book Fire & Blood.
The story is set about 200 years before the events of Game of Thrones and explores the history of House Targaryen, the dragon-riding dynasty that once ruled Westeros. The series focuses on the Targaryen civil war, also known as the Dance of the Dragons, a brutal succession battle that tore the family—and the kingdom—apart.

What I love about the show

House of the Dragon pulled me into a world of fire, power, and complex family dynamics. I love how every character has depth—no one is purely good or evil. The intense political tension, stunning visuals, and of course, the dragons, make it thrilling to watch. The music, costumes, and storytelling feel rich and immersive. It’s not just a fantasy show; it’s a deep exploration of ambition, loyalty, and legacy—and that’s what makes it unforgettable.
